The annular pump focuses to a Bessel core + rings; the Gaussian options focus to a spot. The probe is always a Gaussian.

3 · Momentum bookkeeping (k-space)

The phonon must carry q = kpump − kprobe. The amber side of the triangle is the phonon; its length sets the frequency.

2 · The grating = the phonon it drives (x–z slice through the focus, ≈6 µm wide)

Red = compression, blue = rarefaction of the acoustic wave. Counter-prop gives fine fringes across the beam axis; the annular co-prop case gives a coarser, angle-set grating. Co-prop Gaussian gives a flat field — nothing to drive.

4 · Brillouin shift vs ring angle

Opening the pump ring tunes the co-propagating shift upward from zero; the counter-propagating branch starts high and falls. This continuous tunability via ring angle is the defining feature of your single-objective design.
Isotropic single-mode-acoustic model. fB = (2 n v / λ₀)·sin(θ/2) co-prop, ·cos(θ/2) counter-prop; |q| = 2k·sin(θ/2) or 2k·cos(θ/2), k = 2πn/λ₀, λ₀ = 780.24 nm.
