1 · Noise budget (log photons)

Bars are the three noise terms; the cyan line is the signal. Signal above total noise ⇒ detectable. The tallest bar names your regime.

2 · Brillouin peak vs noise floor

The Lorentzian gain peak (real part) against the shaded noise-equivalent resolution ±σ/N. If the peak clears the band, you resolve a phonon.

3 · Why we modulate — SNR vs integration time

Lock-in @ 100 kHz rides the √τ line down to the shot limit; baseband (DC) saturates on 1/f drift and never reaches it. This is the whole reason the pump is chopped rather than measured at DC.
Signal S = N·(ΔI/I), N = η·P·τ/hν detected photons per time constant.   σshot = √(2N(1+fpump));   σtech = (1−A)·N·RIN·√BW, 1−A = 10−CMRR/20;   σel = √2·NEP·√τ/hν;   σtot = √(σshot²+σtech²+σel²).
Conventions (edit the constants block to match your rig): λ₀ = 780.24 nm, η = 0.85, ENBW = 1/(2τ), baseband 1/f floor δ₁f = 1×10⁻⁴. Prefactors on the noise-equivalent bandwidth are convention-dependent and do not change the regime boundaries or the √τ scaling.
